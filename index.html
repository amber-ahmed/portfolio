About Me

Hi, I am Amber Ahmed. I am 22 years old guy.I am a full stack web developer utilizing MERN stack technology.I live in Hyderabad, India. I was born and raised in Hyderabad, India. I completed my high school(8th-10th) at Central Public High School, Hyderabad. I am interested in new technologies, modernization, and solving problems using technologies. The world was being modernized by software technologies, Software was becoming the solution to everything, these things got me interested in software So I thought of becoming a software engineer. So, After High school, I completed 3 years of diploma in CME(Computer Engineering) from Quli Qutub Shah Polytechnic College and took admitted to Deccan College of Engineering and Technology for graduating  B.E(Bachelor of Engineering) with CSE(Computer Science Engineering) stream. I am in 4th of 4 years of B.E. It is the last year of my graduation.I learnt full stack web development from CFI(Code for India Foundation).



   When I got admitted to a diploma program, I had no prior knowledge of the software field. Unfortunately, the teachers and lecturers lacked the necessary skills and were not very friendly or supportive, making it challenging for me to seek guidance. While I excelled in subjects like math, physics, chemistry, and drawing, I struggled with core subjects like C, C++, and Data Structures, as they were entirely new to me.

   During this time, I acquired my first computer with specifications including a Core Duo CPU, 500GB HDD, 2GB RAM, and Wi-Fi. Motivated to learn, I began searching for resources online and started learning C programming through YouTube. However, due to academic commitments and limited exposure, it took me longer to grasp the fundamentals of the C language. Nonetheless, I managed to complete the basics and even developed my first project, a console GUI tic-tac-toe game in C.

   I also gained some familiarity with C++ along the way. My knowledge of programming surpassed even that of the top students in my class, as the Indian education system tends to prioritize grades and marks over practical skills. In my second year, I encountered a subject called mobile application development, which was my first real exposure to how programming could be used in real-life applications. We used Java and Android Studio for mobile development.

   To accommodate the resource-intensive nature of Android Studio, I upgraded my computer's RAM to 4GB. Despite the limitations, I delved into Android development and blindly started coding a tic-tac-toe game for the Android platform. Eventually, I managed to complete it. During this period, I also developed a simple application called "Secret Notes," which allowed users to store text with a password, essentially functioning as a notepad.

   Regrettably, I made the mistake of assuming my basic knowledge was sufficient at the time and postponed further learning to my college years. Unfortunately, the COVID-19 pandemic struck, and I wasted two years during the subsequent lockdown. On the academic front, I completed my diploma, which granted me direct admission to the second year of a B.E. program.

   The second year and half of the third year were spent in lockdown, where the teachers and lecturers continued to lack sufficient expertise. They taught software engineering in a traditional manner, often relying on blackboards. Additionally, the engineering college still employed outdated software such as Windows 7 in 2021. During this period, I made the mistake of starting to learn new languages or subjects as they arose, only to abandon them once they were covered in the curriculum. Consequently, my understanding of various languages and subjects remained incomplete.

   However, during the third year, we were assigned a mini project. While most of my colleagues resorted to purchasing, borrowing, or copying projects from the internet, I preferred not to engage in such practices. Instead, I decided to extend my "Secret Notes" application, investing four months in coding. Through this project, named "Flashcard," I obtained a deep understanding of Java concepts such as classes, objects, inheritance, packages, and interfaces. While I had become quite proficient with Android Studio, I still did not consider myself a full-fledged Android developer, as I had not explored more advanced features like APIs or camera integration.

   Feeling a sense of achievement, I uploaded "Flashcard" to the Google Play Store, integrating advertisements to potentially earn money. However, given the number of existing competitors and the lack of visibility for my application in search results, my excitement waned, and I abandoned the project as it stood. It was then that I realized the flaws in my journey and approach. Merely creating such applications would not guarantee employment opportunities, and I needed to choose a specific technology or language to focus on. Unfortunately, I lacked proper guidance or a roadmap.

   To rectify this, I began researching different technologies and their applications in the tech industry. I discovered various fields, such as web development, mobile development, desktop development, artificial intelligence, machine learning, blockchain, and data science. Web development caught my interest due to its high demand in the market, the widespread use of web browsers, and the shift towards web-based business management instead of desktop applications. Furthermore, web development appeared comparatively easier to learn and offered promising career prospects. Additionally, my inclination toward building integrated applications aligned well with this field.

   With a resolute decision, I embarked on the path of learning full-stack web development. I followed roadmaps provided by different websites, completing HTML and CSS before commencing JavaScript. As the end of my third year approached, I temporarily paused my learning journey to focus on exam preparations. It became evident that I couldn't undertake this journey alone and needed to enroll in an online or offline web development course. Thus, I began my search for coaching options.

   During this search, I stumbled upon an article on my Google news feed about the "Code for India Foundation" (CFI), an organization offering courses in data analysis and full-stack web development. Impressed by their genuine promises of placement guidance, excellent infrastructure, and a clear syllabus, I decided to visit their website and meet with them. Moreover, the foundation was affiliated with the reputable boot camp known as the Hacking School. After successfully passing the entrance test, I secured admission to CFI.

   Since the previous batch had not yet completed, I had to wait for my own batch to commence. In the meantime, my fourth year of college began. When my full-stack web development batch finally started at CFI, I joined enthusiastically. It has been approximately one month since I joined, and I have gained a wealth of knowledge. I have learned about topics such as number systems, Linux commands, Git, and GitHub. I actively share my progress on LinkedIn, updating my network on what I have learned.

   Now, as a full-stack web developer, I reflect upon my journey so far, realizing the mistakes I made and the need for proper guidance. It is clear that the path I was previously on would not lead me to success. The software industry demands specialization, and with numerous languages and technologies available, a focused approach is essential. I am determined to continue my learning journey in full-stack web development, and I am grateful for the opportunity provided by CFI.
